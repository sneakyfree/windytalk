The core idea

Decouple the three things that are always welded together.

Everywhere else, the voice, the agent, and the compute ship as one locked bundle. Windy Talk pulls them apart. Because they're separate, Windy Talk becomes a membrane that snaps onto anything — any agent above it, any brain beneath it, any surface around it.

That's the Swiss Army knife. Not one blade — a socket everything plugs into.

The founding principle

You get it downloaded. The agent does the rest.

We don't have to anticipate every machine, model, and setup — we just have to get Windy Talk onto the device. Every feature is exposed as a programmable control, and Windy Talk ships as an MCP server, so the user's own agent — their Opus, Fable, Grok, or Gemini instance — puts its hands on all the knobs and finishes the setup itself.

The same surface that lets an agent configure Windy Talk is the surface that lets it bolt onto anything. Agent-operable by design.

Everywhere — mobile included

If it doesn't live in your pocket, it doesn't count.

Windows, macOS (Apple Silicon & Intel), Linux — and the phone, where everyone actually is. On desktop the hands are local. On mobile the OS won't let an app drive other apps, so the hands move to the cloud: the phone version is talk to your agent, and it acts through Windy Hand. That's why the conversation layer is the portable core, and why always-on wake is a desktop luxury and a push-to-talk on the phone.

How it pays — without the liability

Sell the software, not the compute.

Reselling frontier APIs at markup means eating their bills — thin margin, cash-flow risk. So the money is the software: a subscription for the connection and the gating. Compute stays the user's problem — their frontier key, their local model (a $99 "we set it up" one-time), or Windy's own compute as an opt-in, priced above cost, once it's competitive.

And Windy Talk is the front door: the low-friction thing people download that pulls them into Windy agents, chat, clone, cloud, and email.

The proving ground

Integrate the whole ecosystem as if it were third-party.

Windy Talk's first ~20 integrations are Windy's own products — Word, Chat, Agent, Email, Word mobile, Windy Hand — connected through the same public sockets any outsider would use, with no privileged internal hooks. If it can bolt onto all of Windy as a stranger, it can bolt onto anything. The ecosystem is both the test harness and the surface area.
